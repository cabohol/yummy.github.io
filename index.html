Quick and Easy Pancit

Ingredients

  • 1 (12 ounce) package dried rice noodles
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups diced cooked chicken breast meat
  • 1 small head cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 4 carrots, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 lemons - cut into wedges, for garnish

Procedures

Step 1: Gather all ingredients.

Step 2: Place rice noodles in a large bowl; cover with warm water and let soften for 8 to 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.

Step 3: Meanwhile, heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-low heat. Add onion and garlic; cook and stir until onion is tender, about 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in chicken, cabbage, carrots, and soy sauce. Cook until cabbage begins to soften..

Step 4: Toss in noodles and cook, stirring constantly, until heated through.

Step 5: Transfer pancit to a serving dish and garnish with lemon wedges.

Pork Fried Rice

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 tablespoons peanut oil (or vegetable oil) (*Footnote 1)
  • 1/2 lbs (225 g) ground pork
  • 3 tablespoons oyster sauce, divided
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce + 1/2 dark soy sauce) (*Footnote 2)
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed frozen vegetables (carrots, peas, and corn)
  • 3 cups leftover steamed rice
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil

Procedures

Step 1: Gather all ingredients. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground pork, breaking it apart. Cook until browned, about 2 to 3 minutes.

Step 2: Stir in the green onion, garlic, and 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce. Cook for 1 minute to mix well.

Step 3: Add the cooked rice and reduce heat to medium. Stir to combine for 1 to 2 minutes, adding more oil if the rice looks dry. Pour in the remaining 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce and soy sauce. Stir to mix.

Step 4: Add the mixed vegetables and stir until they are defrosted, about 1 minute. Move everything to one side of the pan and add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil. Pour in the beaten eggs, let them set for a few seconds, then scramble and mix with the other ingredients.

Step 5: Taste the rice and add salt to adjust seasoning, mixing well. If desired, let the rice sit in the hot pan for 20 to 30 seconds without stirring for a slightly crispy texture. Add the sesame oil and mix again. Transfer the fried rice onto serving plates and serve hot as a main or side dish.

Chili Garlic Shrimp

Ingredients

  • Shrimp:
  • 1 lb (450 g) raw shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • 1 teaspoon Shaoxing wine (Optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • Sauce:
  • 1/4 cup chili garlic sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons light soy sauce (or soy sauce)
  • Stir Fry:
  • 4 1/2 tablespoons peanut oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger

Procedures

Step 1: Combine the shrimp and Shaoxing wine in a medium-sized bowl. Sprinkle with salt and mix until evenly dispersed. Set aside while preparing the rest of the ingredients.

Step 2: In another bowl, combine all the sauce ingredients and stir until the brown sugar is dissolved. Set aside.

Step 3: Heat 4 tablespoons of oil in a medium-sized skillet until hot. Add the cornstarch and all-purpose flour to the shrimp bowl and toss until fully coated. Shake off any extra flour and add the shrimp to the pan, cooking in batches if necessary. Cook without touching for 2 to 3 minutes until golden, then flip and cook the other side for another 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer cooked shrimp to a large plate without overlapping.

Step 4: Once all shrimp are cooked, turn off the stove and wipe the pan with paper towels to remove any residue. Add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon of oil to the pan and heat over medium heat. Add the minced ginger and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Step 5: Add the sauce to the pan and cook, stirring until the sauce becomes thick and glossy, about 20 to 30 seconds. Turn off the heat and return the shrimp to the pan, tossing to coat thoroughly. Serve hot as a main dish.

Chicken And Broccoli

Ingredients

  • Meat and Marinade:
  • 1 lb (450 g) boneless skinless chicken breast (or thigh)
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Sauce:
  • 3 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1/2 tablespoon dark soy sauce (or soy sauce)
  • 1/4 cup chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Stir-fry:
  • 1 head broccoli, chopped into bite-sized florets
  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon minced ginger

Procedures

Step 1: Slice the chicken against the grain into thin bite-size pieces, no thicker than 1/4” (1/2 cm). Transfer to a medium-sized bowl and add the marinade ingredients. Stir to mix well and let sit for 10 minutes while preparing the other ingredients.

Step 2: Combine all the sauce ingredients in a bowl and stir to mix well.

Step 3: Bring 1/3 cup of water to a boil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the broccoli and cover. Steam until just tender and the water evaporates, about 40 to 50 seconds. Transfer the broccoli to a plate and wipe the pan with a paper towel if any water is left.

Step 4: Add the oil to the pan and swirl to coat the bottom. Spread the chicken in a single layer and cook without touching for 30 seconds, or until the bottom is browned. Flip and cook the other side briefly. Stir until the surface is lightly charred and the inside is still pink. Add the garlic and ginger, stirring a few times to release their flavors.

Step 5: Return the broccoli to the pan. Stir the sauce to dissolve the cornstarch completely and pour it into the skillet. Cook and stir until the sauce thickens, about 1 minute. Transfer everything to a plate immediately and serve hot with steamed rice or boiled noodles as a main dish.
